Essentials: First 24 Hours

Your story begins: The world is quiet. Dust settles. What you do now shapes your future.

  • Mindset: Stay calm. Fear wastes energy. Set tiny, achievable goals. Breathe.
  • Assess: Are you hurt? Is it safe to stay here? Listen. Smell smoke or chemicals?
  • Inventory: What do you have? A knife, a backpack, a lighter? Every item matters.

Shelter: Before Night Falls

Story: The sun is sinking. Wind chills. You need protectionâ€”fast.

  • Scout: Look for abandoned cars, houses, caves. Avoid flood zones and unstable buildings.
  • Safety: Test the ground floor for hazards. Block wind with debris. Keep a clear exit.
  • Insulation: Use leaves, newspapers, or clothing. Stay dry. Damp kills.
  • Signal: Mark your location (bright cloth, stones) if rescue is possible.

Water: Every Drop Matters

Story: You wake, lips cracked. No waterâ€”panic rises. You remember: three days without water is the limit.

  • Sources: Rainwater, clear flowing streams, dew on plastic or leaves.
  • Purification: Boil for 5+ minutes, use improvised filters (sand, cloth), or purifying tablets.
  • Containers: Clean bottles, cooking pots, even plastic bags in a pinch.
  • Warning: Avoid water near factories, roads, or with a chemical smell.
  • Scenario:
    • "Clouds gather. You set out metal pans, catching rain. Later, you sip, grateful and alive."

Food: Find, Forage, Survive

Story: Your stomach growls. Energy dips. But wisdom: you can last 3 weeks without food if you have water.

  • Quick wins: Canned goods, energy bars, dried grains in abandoned homes.
  • Nature: Learn 2-3 edible local plants (dandelion, clover, wild onions).
  • Forage safely: Avoid mushrooms and berries unless certain.
  • Protein: Insects are safe if cooked; avoid bright colors or foul smells.
